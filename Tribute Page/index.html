Dr. Norman Borluag

Dr. Norman Ernest Borlaug (1914–2009) was an American agronomist and humanitarian whose pioneering work in developing high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties revolutionized global agriculture.

His efforts are credited with saving over a billion people from starvation, earning him the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970. Borlaug’s methods laid the foundation for what became known as the Green Revolution, transforming food production in developing nations.

He continued advocating for agricultural innovation and education throughout his life, emphasizing that science and compassion together can change the world.
